The Beatles

The Beatles were an English rock band formed in Liverpool in 1960. The group were integral to the development of 1960s counterculture and popular music's recognition as an art form.

The Rolling Stones

are an English rock band formed in London in 1962. The Rolling Stones have assimilated various musical genres into their own collective sound. Rolling Stone magazine ranked them fourth on the «100 Greatest Artists of All Time» list .

Jimi Hendrix

was an American rock guitarist, singer, and songwriter. The Experience's Rock and Roll Hall of Fame biography states: «Jimi Hendrix was arguably the greatest instrumentalist in the history of rock music».

Bob Dylan

is an American singer-songwriter, author, and visual artist who has been a major figure in popular culture for more than 50 years. Dylan has been described as one of the most influential figures of the 20th century, musically and culturally.

Led Zeppelin

were an English rock band formed in London in 1968. Led Zeppelin's music was rooted in the blues. Many critics consider Led Zeppelin one of the most successful, innovative, and influential rock groups in history.
